01 · Decision frame

Should the assistant write conclusions—or protect the path to evidence?

Sponsor: research operations · review date: 18 Sep Decision: fund an eight-week prototype or pause Primary users: six-person product research teams Success: faster synthesis without lower source confidence

Consequence · This framing determines which product gets funded.

02 · Risk hypothesis

Fluent summaries may erase disagreement before the team sees it.

Observed pain: analysts lose 5–7 hours to evidence sorting Critical risk: a polished claim outranks a contradictory source Disconfirming signal: experts already verify every citation Do not optimize: prose volume or generic answer speed

Consequence · False confidence can turn one weak quote into roadmap certainty.

03 · Research plan

Test traceable synthesis against the team’s current evidence wall.

12 moderated sessions · researchers and product leads Task: reconcile 24 mixed-support interview excerpts Measure: time, citation opens, corrections, confidence shifts Stop condition: two unsupported claims survive review

Consequence · The stop condition can cancel the proposed build.

04 · Guardrail proposal

Generate claims only when the supporting and opposing sources stay attached.

Every claim opens its exact source span Contradictory evidence remains visible, never averaged away Uncertainty language follows evidence coverage Human approval required before export or stakeholder share

Consequence · Reviewability is a release requirement, not a later feature.

05 · Recommendation

Proceed with assisted synthesis. Do not ship autonomous conclusions.

Fund: evidence clustering, contradiction view, cited draft Exclude: final recommendations and source-free summaries Gate 1: 30% faster with no rise in unsupported claims Gate 2: reviewers recover every source in under 10 seconds

Consequence · Approve the prototype; withhold production commitment.
